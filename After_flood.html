After the Flood — Essential Steps for Safety & Recovery

Steps:

1. Wait for Official Clearance

  • Do not return home until authorities say it is safe.
  • Floodwater may hide snakes, insects, open manholes, or sharp objects.
  • Roads may be unstable or deeply eroded even if water looks shallow.


2. Ensure Safety Before Entering the House

Check outside first for:

  • Weak walls
  • Tilted structures
  • Cracks
  • Smell of gas
  • Hanging electric wires
  • Enter slowly and carefully — structures weaken after soaking in water.
  • If you smell gas → leave immediately and call authorities.


3. Clean and Disinfect Thoroughly
  • Remove standing water as soon as possible.
  • Clean mud and dirt with hot water + detergent first (this removes germs).
  • Then disinfect with bleach solution on allowed surfaces.
  • Keep doors/windows open to reduce humidity.


4. Discard Unsafe Items

Throw away:

  • All food touched by water
  • Medicines
  • Cosmetics
  • Soap & shampoo bottles contaminated by floodwater
  • Discard porous items (sofas, mattresses, carpets) if wet for 24–48 hours.
  • Wooden toys, books, cushions → usually cannot be saved.


5. Protect Yourself During Cleanup

Wear:

  • Heavy gloves
  • Long boots
  • Mask
  • Long clothing
  • Avoid touching eyes, nose, mouth during cleaning.
  • Wash hands thoroughly afterward.


6. Document Damage for Assistance

Take clear photos of:

  • Furniture
  • Walls
  • Electric appliances
  • Broken windows/doors
  • Record everything you discard.
  • This helps in claiming insurance, NGO relief, or government support.


7. Prevent Mold Growth

Mold grows within 24 hours, so act fast:

  • Open windows for ventilation
  • Use fans if electricity is safe
  • Sun-dry items
  • Clean small mold areas with bleach solution
  • If mold covers a large area → call professionals; it is dangerous to inhale.


8. Check Electric & Gas Lines

Before turning anything ON:

  • Have the electrical system inspected
  • Check fuse box for water exposure
  • Test appliances only after drying completely
  • Gas leaks can occur in floods → get lines inspected too.


9. Ensure Drinking Water Safety
  • Tap water may be contaminated after floods.
  • Until official confirmation:
    • Use bottled water
    • OR boil water for 3–5 minutes
    • OR use reliable purification tablets


10. Community Action
  • Help clear drains and streets.
  • Assist elderly neighbors.
  • Report clogged sewage lines to authorities.
  • Share extra water, food, or clothing if you can.


11. Keep Emergency Numbers Accessible
  • Rescue helpline
  • Local police
  • Medical emergency
  • Electricity & gas company helplines
  • Maintain these numbers in phone contact list or a small written card in your emergency bag


Remember

Do what you can, stay safe, and trust Allah’s protection in every stage of recovery.
