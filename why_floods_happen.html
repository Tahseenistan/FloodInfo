Why do Floods happen?

Floods happen when more water enters an area than the land or drainage system can safely handle. The major causes include:

1. Heavy Rainfall

When it rains continuously or very heavily, the ground cannot absorb all the water.
This raises river levels and causes water to spread into nearby areas.



2. Melting Glaciers

In summer, higher temperatures melt glaciers in the northern areas of Pakistan.
This melted water flows into rivers, increasing their volume and sometimes causing overflow.



3. Poor Drainage & Deforestation

Cutting trees reduces the land's ability to absorb water.
Also, blocked drains and unplanned construction prevent water from flowing away properly — leading to urban flooding.



4. Release of Water from Upstream Countries

Sometimes, when dams or barrages in upstream regions (like India) release excess water, it raises water levels in Pakistan’s rivers.
This can lead to sudden rises in flow and possible flooding.



5. River Overflow

When rivers swell due to rain, glacier melt, or water release, they cross their banks and spread into surrounding villages and cities.
